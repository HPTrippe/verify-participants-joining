What you get from being part of the Identity and Attributes Exchange (IAX)

Sell products and services to the UK government

The IAX is currently the only digital identity scheme that lets you sell your products and services to the UK government.

Save time, effort and money

Your organisation will get a trust mark when you become a trusted provider in the IAX scheme. This proves your organisation follows government and industry guidance on how to:

  • stop criminals or users that are pretending to be someone else
  • run a secure product or service

The trust mark will also prove your product or service can be used safely. This can make it easier to sell it to potential buyers.

Show other schemes you meet their standards

Organisations that are part of schemes in other industries (such as finance, health and retail) might be certified against equivalent standards. The trust mark will show that you've already been certified.

This can reduce the time and effort you'd usually spend on preparing to go through the certification process. This can cost some organisations up to £45,000. In some cases it can even mean you will not need to go through another full certification process.

Make it easier for users to do things online

You'll know exactly how other organisations on the IAX scheme have created their digital identities or attributes because you’ll meet the same standards. You can trust that any information you get from other participants is accurate and reliable.

Sharing identities and attributes with other organisations will ultimately make it easier for users to do things online. This is because they'll no longer need to prove their identity and eligibility every time they interact with a new organisation.
