How certification works on the IAX

Participants will need to prove they meet all the standards and rules of the Identity and Attributes Exchange (IAX).

You’ll do this by answering questions about how your organisation works and providing evidence of any existing certificates you have.

How many questions you’ll be asked depends on the:

  • role you want to perform on the IAX
  • level of certification you want to achieve
  • level of risk associated with your service
  • level of assurance you want to provides

Stages of certification

The certification process is in 2 stages.

Firstly, you’ll need to complete an application form which covers:

  • details about your organisation
  • all the relevant standards you’ll need to meet
  • information about your service

Once an auditor has assessed your application you can move on to the second stage.

You’ll then need to provide more detailed technical information in:

  • the base criteria document
  • any other relevant criteria document
  • the service specification document

You’ll also be asked to provide evidence that proves you meet all the relevant standards.

You’ll need to choose an auditor to work with and they’ll help to guide you through the assessment process.

Step 1: check your eligibility

To be eligible to apply for certification, you’ll need to:

  • have built your service
  • follow a quality management system, for example ISO 9001 or equivalent
  • follow a recognised security standard, for example ISO 27001 or equivalent
  • follow data protection rules and regulations, for example GDPR
  • follow internationally recognised risk management standards, such as ISO/IEC 31000, ISO/IEC 27005
  • carry out internal audits
  • keep audit records and security logs - these should include dates and times of any system compromises and what you did to fix them
  • have someone on your management team who's responsible for security policies and systems

If you’re unsure if you meet any of these, contact the authoritative body [LINK] for more information.

Step 2: apply for certification

Complete the application form and return it to the authoritative body. The application form contains details about:

  • your organisation
  • your service
  • how you meet the IAX’s standards and rules

Your application form will be reviewed by the authoritative body to ensure you meet the IAX’s standards and rules. You can then move on to the assessment stage.

Once you know you can continue, you should choose an auditor from the IAX’s approved list.

Your auditor will:

  • guide you through the assessment process
  • confirm the level of assessment you need, this will be either approval or full certification
  • decide if you pass or fail assessment your service

Step 3: get assessed

Your auditor will ask you for any more evidence they need to see. It’s their job to make sure you meet all the relevant standards.

You’ll need to involve different people in your organisation, for instance technical and compliance experts.

You’ll be asked to provide more technical and detailed information about your service and organisation, including:

  • a detailed description of your service and how it meets relevant government standards, for example ‘How to prove and verify someone’s identity’
  • how your organisation operates, for instance how you manage risk and data security
  • technical diagrams outlining how your service works

You’ll also need to gather and send the auditor any evidence they want to see. They’ll advise you where you need to provide more evidence or information.

The assessment may involve an on-site audit. Your auditor will let you know if you need this. The assessment stage normally takes about 3 weeks.

Step 4: joing the IAX

When your auditor has made a decision they’ll let you know and also pass their pass or fail recommendation to the authoritative body.

Before you can join the IAX you may be asked to adapt your service to meet the relevant standards.

Once the authoritative body has confirmed the auditor’s positive assessment, you should:

  • sign the contract
  • agree to the IAX’s terms and conditions
  • start using the IAX’s trust mark
  • join the IAX
