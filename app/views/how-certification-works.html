How certification works

Your organisation must be 'certified' before you can join the trust framework.

To be certified, an independent auditor will need to check that you can provide identity services that meet all the necessary standards and rules. You’ll need to choose an auditor you’d like to work with.

It usually takes between 1 and 3 months to be certified. It costs £0000000000000 to become a member of the trust framework.

Levels of certification

You’ll need a different level of certification depending on your role in the trust framework. Depending on your role, and its complexity, you’ll need to either:

  • send evidence of your compliance to your auditor
  • send evidence of your compliance to your auditor and host an on-site audit
  • send evidence of your compliance to your auditor and host 2 on-site audits

Your auditor will let you know the level of certification you need. Based on the evidence you provide, your auditor will then decide if you meet the relevant identity standards.

Get certified

To get certified you need to:

  • apply to join the trust framework
  • choose an auditor
  • gather evidence about your service to send to your auditor
  • if necessary, host an auditor at your place of work
