How certification works

Your organisation must be 'certified' before you can join the Identity and Attributes Exchange (IAX).

To be certified, an independent auditor will need to check that you can provide identity services that meet all the necessary standards and rules. You might be able to use certificates you already have to count towards your certification.

The authoritative body will appoint an auditor to work with you.

It usually takes between 1 and 3 months to be certified.

Benefits of certification

Being certified will:

  • make it easier for users to create a digital identity they trust
  • allow providers to use the trust mark to show users they're secure and trusted
  • create a benchmark of trust which will allow different services and organisations to work together with confidence

Levels of certification

You’ll need a different level of certification depending on your role in the IAX.

Your auditor will determine the level of certification you need.

The level of certification you need is based on the:

  • kind of service or product you want to provide
  • level of risk associated with your service or product
  • level of assurance you want to provide

Generally, the higher these factors the higher the level of certification you’ll need. There are 3 levels of certification available. You’ll need to do one of the following:

  • send evidence of your compliance to your auditor
  • send evidence of your compliance to your auditor and host an on-site audit
  • send evidence of your compliance to your auditor and host 2 on-site audits

The cost, and the time it takes to be certified, rises with each certification level. Based on the evidence, your auditor will determine if you meet the relevant identity standards.

Get certified

To get certified you need to:

  • apply to join the IAX
  • gather evidence about your service to send to your auditor
  • if necessary, host an auditor at your place of work

Get certified by an auditor to join the IAX.
