Title

Sometimes users will need to prove they are who they say they are to do something online.

There isn't currently a consistent way for users to do this. This can cause problems for teams across the public and private sectors. They might need to buy a way to check their users' identities for each service they have. They can end up paying different prices for very similar identity checking products.

Although these identity checking products might do the same thing, they won't always give a user an identity account that they can use again to access other services. This means users will often need to start from scratch and prove their identity every time they interact with a new organisation online.

Inconsistencies in the way identities are checked online can be exploited by fraudsters. This can lead to identity fraud, which costs government alone between £1 billion and £4 billion a year.

The new trust framework can help solve these problems. By following the guidance and standards included in the trust framework, you'll be able to build an identity checking product that's secure and can work with similar products and services across the public and private sectors.

Title

There are different ways you can be a supplier in the trust framework. You can do any of the following:

  • check users' information against authoritative data sources by becoming an 'attribute provider'
  • check users' identities and sell their accounts by becoming an 'identity provider'
  • connect multiple attribute providers and identity providers to services by becoming a 'broker'

How much you earn from being part of the trust framework will depend on which product or service you choose to supply. For example, if you're an identity provider you'll get paid each time a user:

  • creates a digital identity account using your product
  • signs in to a public or private sector service using their digital identity account

What else you get from being part of the trust framework

Being part of the trust framework will give you the chance to:

  • access the digital identity market
  • find out about potential threats to your product by sharing threat intelligence and fraud monitoring information with other organisations
  • sell more trustworthy products by following best practice guidance on identity proofing
