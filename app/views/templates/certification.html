Get certified to be part of the trust framework

You’re ready to be certified once you’ve:

  • decided on the role you want to play in the trust framework
  • applied to join the trust framework
  • chosen an auditor you want to work with
  • built your service

Before you start

Your auditor will need to confirm you meet some conditions before you carry on with your application.

They’ll ask you to complete a statement of conformity. The statement of conformity covers things like:

  • a brief description of your service to help your auditor understand your service
  • evidence you meet the necessary criteria, for example your company is registered with Companies House

Download the statement of conformity

Your auditor will let you know if you can carry on with your application, or if you need to provide more evidence.

Complete the application documents

You’ll now need to provide more information so your auditor can evaluate your application for certification.

You’ll need to:

  • write a full description of your service
  • complete the certification criteria report which outlines how you meet all of the necessary standards and rules

Your auditor will advise you on the areas you need to provide more detail about in the documentation.

Download the certification criteria report

Send evidence and information about your service to your auditor

Send your completed application documentation to your auditor. The auditor may need to ask you some follow up questions about the evidence you’ve submitted.

Your auditor will now decide if they’ll recommend you’ve passed the audit or if an on-site audit is necessary.

On-site audits are needed because of the complexity of the role you’ll play on the trust framework. Some roles will need 2 on-site audits. Your auditor will advise you on what’s necessary in your case.

Getting certified

Once the audit process is complete, the auditor will let you know if they’ll be recommending a pass or fail.

If they recommend a pass, they’ll submit the evidence and their decision to the authoritative body. The authoritative body will review the evidence and confirm if they agree with the decision.

If you’ve failed, your auditor will suggest what you can do now. For instance, they may arrange another on-site audit or ask you to provide further evidence.

Once the authoritative body has confirmed you’ve passed, you’re ready to sign the contract with the trust framework.
