Get certified to be part of the trust framework

Read the guidance about how certification works before you start the process. This includes why you should be certified and what you'll need.

You can then:

  • find out what evidence you need to be certified (includes what information you need to prove you meet each pre-assessment element)
  • read the terms and conditions of certification (includes the different types of certification, information about the auditor's report and how to respond to their decision)
  • complete a statement of conformity to prove you meet the standards
  • find out when you can and cannot use the trust framework trustmark

Choose how you will be certified

There are 3 ways your organisation can be certified. Which guidance you need to follow will depend on which one you choose.

If you want to do most of the audit yourself (known as 'self-assessment'), you will need to know:

  • what you need
  • how it works

If you want the 'approval' level of certification, you will need to know:

  • what you need
  • how it works

If you want the 'full certification' level of certification, you will need to know:

  • what you need
  • how it works

Profiles

You must make sure you meet a profile.

Describe your service

You will need to write a brief description of your service to help your auditor know if you've chosen the right level of certification for your service. Find out what you need to include in the description.

You will also need to write a full description of your service that you can send to auditors and the authoritative body. Find out what you need to include in the description.

Testing your service

What you need to do before and after your service is certified: service design, service transition, service operations, security testing, penetration testing, business continuity, event and capacity management.
