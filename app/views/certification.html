Get certified to be part of the IAX

You’re ready to be certified once you’ve:

  • decided on the role you want to play in the IAX
  • applied to join the IAX
  • chosen an auditor you want to work with
  • built your service

Before you start your application

Your auditor will need to confirm you meet some conditions before you carry on with your application.

They’ll ask you to complete a statement of conformity. The statement of conformity covers things like:

  • a brief description of your service to help your auditor understand your service
  • evidence you meet the necessary criteria, for example your company is registered with Companies House

Your auditor will use it to let you know if you can apply for certification, confirm the level you need, or if you need to provide more evidence.

They do this using the criteria outlined in the documents on Audit a service that wants to join the IAX and in the relevant profiles.

Download the statement of conformity

Complete the application documents

You’ll need to provide more information so your auditor can evaluate your application for certification.

You’ll need to:

  • write a full description of your service
  • send your auditor the evidence they ask to see

Your auditor will advise you on the particular areas you need to provide more detail about in your application.

Working with your auditor

The auditor may need to ask you some follow up questions about the evidence you’ve submitted.

Your auditor will decide if they have enough information to make a decision or if an on-site audit is necessary.

On-site audits are needed because of the complexity of the role you’ll play on the IAX. Some roles will need 2 on-site audits. Your auditor will tell you what you need to do and what happens next.

Getting certified

Once the audit process is complete, the auditor will let you know if they’ll be recommending a pass or fail.

If they recommend a pass, they’ll submit the evidence and their decision to the authoritative body. The authoritative body will review the evidence and confirm if they agree with the decision.

If you’ve failed, your auditor will suggest what you can do now. For instance, they may arrange another on-site audit or ask you to provide further evidence.

What's next

Once the authoritative body has confirmed you’ve passed, you’re ready to sign the contract with the IAX and start using the trustmark.
