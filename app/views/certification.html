Get certified to be part of the IAX

You’re ready to be certified once you’ve:

  • decided on the role you want to play in the IAX
  • been given an auditor you want to work with
  • built your service

Before you start certification

Your auditor will need to check you meet some conditions before you can be certified. You’ll need to complete the pre-assessment checklist before they can do this.

If your auditor confirms you meet all the required conditions, you must apply to join the IAX. You're now ready to be certified.

You can read more about the certification process (Google Docs).

Complete a statement of conformity

Your auditor will ask you to complete a statement of conformity. The statement of conformity includes:

  • a brief description of your service
  • evidence you meet the necessary criteria, for example your company is registered with Companies House
  • evidence that you follow a recognised risk management standard

Your auditor will use this information to tell you if you:

  • need to provide more evidence before you can apply
  • can make the certification process quicker using certificates you already have or regulations you already meet

Download the statement of conformity

Complete your application

You’ll need to provide more information so your auditor can evaluate your application.

You’ll need to:

  • write a full description of your service (Google Docs) and send it to your auditor
  • send your auditor any other evidence they ask to see

The auditor will also assess your technical competence and the controls you have in place. They'll do this using profiles. You’ll be assessed against the identity provider profile (Google Docs) and the base profile (Google Docs).

The profiles list the evidence you need to provide to pass certification.

Your auditor will:

  • advise on any areas in your application that you need to provide more detail about
  • check you comply with the IAX's standards and rules

Working with your auditor

The auditor may need to ask you some follow up questions about the evidence you’ve submitted.

Your auditor will decide if they have enough information to make a decision or if an onsite audit is necessary.

Onsite audits are needed because of the complexity of the role you’ll play on the IAX. Some roles will need 2 onsite audits.

Given the current situation, wherever possible the auditor will do a remote audit and desk review of the evidence you supply and arrange a remote meeting as a follow up.

Your auditor will tell you what you need to do and what happens next.

Getting certified

Once the audit process is complete, the auditor will let you know if they’ll be recommending a pass or fail.

If they recommend a pass, they’ll submit the evidence and their decision to the authoritative body. The authoritative body will review the evidence and confirm if they agree with the decision.

If you’ve failed, your auditor will suggest what you can do now. For instance, they may arrange another onsite audit or ask you to provide further evidence.

What's next

Once the authoritative body has confirmed you’ve passed, you’re ready to sign the contract with the IAX and start using the trustmark.
